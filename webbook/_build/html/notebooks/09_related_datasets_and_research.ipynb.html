Related Datasets and Research Resources#

Overview#

The Turkish Delight Corpus (TDC) is part of a broader ecosystem of open datasets and computational resources developed for Turkish makam music research.

Although this book primarily focuses on the computational analysis of SymbTr v3.0, several complementary datasets and research projects have been developed to support phrase segmentation, melodic analysis, symbolic music processing, and Music Information Retrieval (MIR).

This chapter introduces these related datasets together with representative research studies that provide additional context for computational musicology and machine learning applications in Turkish makam music.

The Turkish Makam Research Ecosystem#

Computational research on Turkish makam music has evolved from symbolic score collections to phrase-level datasets, melodic annotations, and machine learning applications.

The Music Technology Group (MTG), Universitat Pompeu Fabra (UPF), has played a leading role in developing openly available datasets that enable reproducible computational musicology research.

Together, these resources provide a comprehensive ecosystem for symbolic music analysis, phrase segmentation, similarity analysis, clustering, visualization, and machine learning.

SymbTr v3.0#

SymbTr v3.0 is the primary symbolic corpus used throughout this book.

The dataset contains approximately 3,000 symbolic scores representing a wide variety of Turkish makams, usuls, musical forms, and composers.

Throughout this tutorial, SymbTr serves as the foundation for exploratory data analysis, statistical analysis, visualization, dimensionality reduction, clustering, and comparative computational musicology studies.

Turkish Makam Symbolic Phrase Dataset#

The Turkish Makam Symbolic Phrase Dataset extends the SymbTr corpus by providing manually annotated phrase boundaries created by expert musicians.

Instead of complete musical works, the dataset represents phrase-level symbolic units, enabling research on phrase similarity, phrase segmentation, symbolic music retrieval, and computational analysis of melodic structures.

The dataset has become an important benchmark for phrase-based computational musicology.

Turkish Makam Melodic Phrase Dataset#

The Turkish Makam Melodic Phrase Dataset provides manually annotated melodic phrase boundaries extracted from symbolic music scores.

These annotations facilitate research on melodic segmentation, melodic pattern discovery, phrase classification, and machine learning approaches for symbolic music analysis.

Together with the Symbolic Phrase Dataset, it complements the SymbTr corpus by supporting phrase-level computational investigations.

Relationship Between the Datasets#

Although these datasets are closely related, they represent different structural levels of Turkish makam music.

While SymbTr provides complete symbolic scores, the phrase datasets focus on localized musical structures that enable more detailed computational analyses.

Together they establish a unified framework for computational musicology research.

Why Turkish Delight Corpus?#

Unlike conventional dataset repositories, the Turkish Delight Corpus combines openly available datasets with fully reproducible computational workflows.

By integrating exploratory data analysis, statistical methods, visualization, machine learning, clustering, and comparative analysis into a unified Jupyter Book, the project serves both as an educational resource and as a reproducible research platform.

The Turkish Delight Corpus therefore complements existing Turkish makam music datasets by demonstrating not only what data are available but also how they can be analyzed using modern computational techniques.

Open Research Challenges#

Current challenges include

  • melodic similarity learning,

  • explainable machine learning,

How These Resources Complement the Turkish Delight Corpus#

The Turkish Delight Corpus (TDC) does not replace the existing datasets; rather, it complements them by providing a unified and reproducible computational framework.

While previous resources primarily focused on data collection and annotation, TDC emphasizes computational analysis through interactive Jupyter notebooks. The book integrates exploratory data analysis, statistical methods, visualization, dimensionality reduction, clustering, and comparative analyses into a single educational and research-oriented platform.

This integration enables researchers, educators, and students to move seamlessly from accessing symbolic music datasets to performing reproducible computational analyses using modern Python-based workflows.

Evolution of Turkish Makam Music Research#

The development of computational resources for Turkish makam music can be viewed as a progressive evolution.

  • 2012: Introduction of the SymbTr symbolic music corpus.

  • 2014: Release of phrase-level symbolic datasets.

  • 2014: Automatic melodic segmentation using usul and makam information.

  • 2014: Audio–score alignment methodologies.

  • 2025: SymbTr v3.0 expands the corpus to approximately 3,000 symbolic scores and provides additional machine-readable representations.
